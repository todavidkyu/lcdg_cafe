History of the Cafe


The word “Gailana” was a duchess consort of Thuringia by marriage to Hedan I and Gozbert. She is known from the hagiography of Saint Kilian. During her second marriage, Kilian came to the duchy as a Christian missionary, converting Gozbert to Christianity. Geilana, however, preferred to remain faithful to Germanic paganism.

In honor of the Franconian Apostle Kilian, who gave our distillery its name, we are presenting the first edition of the limited Kiliani bottling, which is dedicated to GAILANA, the wife of the then Franconian Duke Gosbert.

The special bottling, limited to 1,702 bottles, matured over three years in former Pinot Noir barrels of a renowned Franconian winery and also has a special feature in the distillation method: With three different settings of the reflux condenser, multiple distillation can be achieved.

On the nose, GAILANA is reminiscent of a beguiling rendezvous of fruity aromas of wine gum, strawberry and raspberry. Red gooseberry meets creamy vanilla, light floral notes as well as a touch of fresh mint and a pinch of oak spice.
